Ajir Research Labs: askNoema Artificial Intelligence Policy

Effective Date: June 2026

Review Cycle: Bi-annual

Document Class: Operational AI & Disclosure Standard

1. Preamble & Scope

Ajir Research Labs operates askNoema, a social intelligence platform that functions as an active epistemic layer, helping users reason through short-form media at the moment of consumption. This Artificial Intelligence (AI) Policy outlines the operational rules, data governance structures, and disclosure standards governing all AI analysis within the platform. It serves as a binding mandate for product engineering, user-facing communications, and third-party API integrations.

2. Foundational AI Principles

askNoema’s development and deployment of AI are governed by the following non-negotiable principles:

  • Human Dignity and Epistemic Agency: Artificial intelligence must expand human reasoning capacity, not replace it. askNoema’s AI is designed as a reasoning partner that prompts further human inquiry, serving human dignity over data extraction.
  • Truth as a Standard: The platform will never knowingly facilitate the spread of misinformation. It actively surfaces evidential uncertainty, verified facts, and contested claims irrespective of the media's ideological valence.
  • Transparency: Users have an absolute right to know when they are interacting with an AI system. AI transparency is a structural requirement across all outputs.
  • Responsible Scaling: New AI capabilities, features, or data processing pipelines will only be launched following rigorous safety and ethics reviews.

3. Prohibited Uses of Artificial Intelligence

To ensure safety and align with both the EU AI Act and Kenyan legal guidelines, the following AI applications are strictly prohibited within askNoema and cannot be unlocked by any operator:

  • Synthetic Misinformation: Generating or amplifying synthetic misinformation, or fabricating factual claims presented as truth.
  • Non-Disclosed Media: Producing deepfakes, manipulated audio, or synthetic media of identifiable individuals without explicit disclosure.
  • Source Impersonation: Using AI to impersonate public officials, journalists, institutional sources, or academic researchers.
  • Unconsented Surveillance: Deploying biometric surveillance or emotion recognition on users without informed and revocable consent.
  • Sensitive Profiling: Profiling users based on religion, ethnicity, political opinion, health status, or sexual orientation.
  • Electoral Manipulation: Generating content designed to manipulate electoral processes or civic voting behavior.
  • Unsupervised Moderation: Deploying autonomous content moderation systems that enact user sanctions or content removal without a human review pathway.

4. Misinformation and Veracity Assessment

The proprietary AI reasoning engine, Episteme, handles the assessment of short-form media claims.

  • Assessment Protocol: The AI system systematically extracts factual claims from media, maps primary evidence sources, weighs evidential credibility, and assigns a structured confidence score (High, Medium, Low, or Unverifiable).
  • Intervention Limits: askNoema does not adjudicate genuinely divided expert consensus or remove content from external platforms. It categorizes interventions precisely, applying distinct flags for factual errors, misleading framing, and malinformation.
  • AI Disclosure: Every AI-generated response must display a visible AI disclosure indicator. Furthermore, if analyzed content is suspected of being fully produced by AI, the system will apply a clear 'AI Generation Suspected' label alongside its supporting analysis.

5. Media Literacy and User Autonomy

askNoema treats the facilitation of media literacy as a rights-based obligation rather than a premium feature.

  • Design for Autonomy: Interfaces must prioritize explanation over conclusion, visually distinguish high and low-certainty outputs, and provide one-tap access to primary source citations.
  • Anti-Addictive Interfaces: The platform strictly avoids interaction designs that exploit cognitive vulnerabilities, such as streaks or variable reward loops.
  • Protection of Minors: The platform implements age-appropriate interfaces for users under 18, offering simplified reasoning guides and refusing to target minors for commercial data profiling.

6. Data Privacy and Governance

Ajir Research Labs acts as a Data Controller, maintaining a strict data minimization protocol under the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019.

  • Ephemeral Processing: URLs and media uploads submitted for veracity assessments are processed ephemerally and are not retained beyond the session unless explicitly saved by the user to build continuity across sessions.
  • Session Security: User queries, AI interaction logs, and feed preferences are encrypted in transit and at rest.
  • Strict Prohibition on Data Selling: The platform relies on GDP-compliant cloud infrastructure and explicitly prohibits the selling, licensing, or sharing of personal data with data brokers or advertising networks.
  • User Data Rights: Users maintain full rights to access, correct, delete, object to processing, and port their data. Furthermore, no consequential decisions about a user may be made solely by automated AI processing.

7. Accountability and Human Oversight

  • Human Governance: AI remains a tool under human governance. Ajir Research Labs retains the authority to audit, override, and intervene in any AI output, ensuring it never operates beyond human correction.
  • User Challenge Mechanism: Users have a formalized pathway to contest any AI assessment they deem inaccurate. Submitting a challenge initiates a human review within 7 working days, which can result in the assessment being revised in the public audit trail.
  • Transparency Reporting: Ajir Research Labs will publish an annual Transparency Report disclosing veracity assessment volumes, user challenge outcomes, and detailed summaries of any corrected AI system failures.

8. Operator and Partnership Rules

Organizations integrating askNoema via API or institutional partnerships are bound by these operational standards.

  • Operator Integrity: Operators must preserve AI disclosure obligations for their end-users and are barred from utilizing the platform for mass surveillance, behavioral targeting, or political propaganda.
  • Educational Contexts: University partnerships must incorporate an AI literacy education component. Student data collected under these programs receives heightened protection and cannot be processed for commercial profiling.
